When Ethan came partways down the creek she was standing still at the bend. She was a girl of fourteen years who, later as a woman of thirty years, was responsible for the premature birth of my sister. Later known as Madeline, or Maddie, and sometimes when her friends shouted her name from below the mezzanine, Mad. But when Ethan saw her white dress flowing gently under that light of the creek I couldn't hold her responsible for her coming and going, her drinking, or that vanishing image of her shadow from a closed doorway. He saw her briefly and then all at once when the light gave way and she turned her head.
