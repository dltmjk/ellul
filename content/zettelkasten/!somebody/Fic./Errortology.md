His father was a college professor, erra-tology: the study and ontology of mistake-making. His most famous class 'Err03', mandatory for all freshmen in an administrative attempt to suitably prepare them for future academic careers

briefly mandatory between the summers of 09 and 10 in an 'far-sighted' administrative attempt to future-proof their undergraduate curriculum. Reverted to an elective after freshmen attempted to replicate class content culminating in two separate, but parallel in time, deaths by banana peel and car crash respectively. His father worked in research: his last paper "towards a comprehensive epistemology of error-making" attempted to trace a comprehensive *being* of decision making. Which in a speech, given at the annual CoM (conference of mistakes), he argued "would allow for a reformulation of the world through a vocabulary of mistakes, in essence (he cleared his throat), finding the general ontology of a mistake through the study of its particulars". Following this, his graduate seminar class covered a mix of major and minor historical blunders: Hitler's invasion of Russia; the Crucifixion of Christ; and the accidental poisoning of a colleagues daughter. Proving to be hugely unpopular among both graduate students and faculty, at the end of the semester, there were four remaining: two endlessly masochistic mathematics majors (both in Algebraic Geometry), his father, and K. Still a boy at the time and understanding little to none of his academic parlance employed, he found, in the way youths are drawn along by certain currents, to the general motif of his Father's work.
