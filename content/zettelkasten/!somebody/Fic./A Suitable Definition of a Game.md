Def. Game

Game; Gamen; Gamenian; Gaman; Gamman; Ga - [prefix] - Mann

From the old English german, itself from the Saxon gaman. Relevant Frisian, High-Germanic, Danish, Norse and Swedish all stem from the Proto-Germantic schema of [Word for participation] + [Word for man], e.g.: Gaman

Varro tells us that masters had dominion over flesh and language, choosing the slaves new name as sparingly and carelessly one would choose the colour of a newlybought cloak. Syra from the airy streets of Asia minor, where she was bought; Delia, a Greek name, but taken after the crowded centre of Delos where adjacent her husband stood, was separated, and then made '*Davus*'. After a jester in a comedy.
