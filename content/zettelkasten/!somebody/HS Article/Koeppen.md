Wolfgang Koeppen lived and died in Munich (not true). The bastard son of a Prussian seamstress and eye-doctor, Koeppen jumped from Grandma to sister to aunt and cousin until eighteen, from there odd jobs: bookboy, ticket taker, junior journalist. 

Hoffman describes Koeppen's 'workspace', a room holding four disjoint desks. Each with their own typewriter and each with its hammer and bell halfway down the page, each page unfinished.
