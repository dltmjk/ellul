What is the TT and ST account. 

ST account
1. A matching phase in which I imaginatively adopt your perspective of the world by entertaining your beliefs and desires and by quarantining my beliefs and desires that we do not share. 
2. The simulation phase during which I think, deliberate, and entertain reasons for possible actions from your perspective. 
3. The attribution phase. After ceasing to entertain your perspective, I base my folk psychological interpretation of your action on my knowledge of what happened during the simulation phase

Bailey - Narrative competence requires perspective taking. 

What is perspective taking? 
Quick rundown of bailey position. 

Claim by Gallagher and Hutto: Narrativist accounts need to be "suitably internalised". Narrative understanding cannot appeal to rules, or rote memorisation. Instead, proper internalisation requires an agent to "absorb" a narrative. 

Point: Therefore, what does 'internalisation' consist of? 
I.e., what is the qualitative difference between absorbing and rote memorisation of a narrative? pg. 154

Claim: 
1. Internalisation is the ability for an agent to "actually imaginatively see things in the way that narratives suggest" 
2. We need to be able to imagine relevant parts of the narrative, from another point of view. I.e., perspective taking
3. Furthermore, you must be able to identify the imaginative object and point of view with the correct emotion. For example, understanding why something is scary even if you do not necessarily find it scary yourself. 

This is generally in line with perspective taking. Therefore, a narrative account depends on a perspective taking account.
