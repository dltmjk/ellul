## Machine and Technique
1. A technique is not a machine
	1. However, the machine is the most obvious and impressive example of a technique 
	2. Still, it is incorrect to act as if these two terms are synonyms. 
		1. Dealing with the machine is not the same as dealing with the technique, the technique is now far more developed than the machine
			1. The machine depends upon it, it is a small part of technique. It is the result of technique and the social and economic application of the machine are made possible through the technique. 
	3. The machine is a type of example for which the technique strives. It is almost pure technique 
		1. Its weight topples everything that it was placed on-top of (Industrial Society) and therefore everything had to be reconsidered in terms of it. 
2. This is what technique accomplishes 
	1. It reorganizes society in terms of the machine. 
		> Old houses that were not suited to the workers were torn down; and the new world technique required was built in their place. - The Technological Society, Ellul #quote 
	
		> It clarifies, arranges, and rationalizes; it does in the domain of the abstract what the machine did in the domain of labor. It is efficient and brings efficiency to everything. Moreover, technique is sparing in the use of the machine, which has traditionally been exploited to conceal defects of organization. “Machines sanctioned social inefficiency,” says Mumford. Technique, on the other hand, leads to a more rational and less indiscriminate use of machines. It places machines exactly where they ought to be and requires of them just what they ought to do - The Technological Society, Ellul #quote

	2. This is similar to what [[Heidegger]] was referring to in his Essay Concerning Technology
	3. Henri Guitton proposed that social growth was, hitherto, an unconscious mechanism, 'reflexive'
		> Social growth was formerly reflexive or instinctive, that is to say, unconscious. But new circumstances (the machine) now compel us to recognize a kind of social development that is rational, intelligent, and conscious. We may ask ourselves whether this is the beginning not only of the era of a spatially finite world but also of the era of a conscious world. Henri Guitton #quote

	4. Technique specifies the attitudes which man makes towards the world. These attitudes, or models, are taken as valid. 
		1. When the technique has been integrated into every area in life, it becomes integrated within man.
