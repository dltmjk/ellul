We should **therefore** consider, firstly, **that** all the external senses, **in-sofar as they are parts of the body, even** if we direct them to objects through action, i.e. through **local** motion (pg. 405, added to Ams)

or the origin **itself** of the nerves, **depends upon the imagination itself**, as the common sense depends on external sense. **By this means we may even** understand **clearly** how all the motions of animals can occur—**notwithstanding that we** grant there **is nothing** in them **which is not** purely corporeal—and even how all those operations occur in us which we perform without the **aid** of reason. (pg. 406, added to Cam)
