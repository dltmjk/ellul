This section confused me on my first reading. Citation 87 reads that Descartes still believes in the use of intuition, but admits that it has a limit. His attempt at deriving the Sine Law did not seem like a reductio ad absurdum argument but seemed like a genuine attempt at proving cases of intuitions efficacy. All this is why quotation 96 surprised me. On finishing the subsection, I felt confused on what Descartes thought about intuition, not that intuition itself was a fruitless endeavour. In any case, Descartes true ideas on this are hard to place - not helped by his unconstrained future usage of the term. I feel a little bit more on exploring his outwardly contradictory positions would've helped me before leading into his rejection in Le Monde.
