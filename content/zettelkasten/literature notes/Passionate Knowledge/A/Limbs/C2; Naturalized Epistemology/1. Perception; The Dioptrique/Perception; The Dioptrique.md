T2: 
	"Descartes never veers" 
	He still carries with him the method and mode of argumentation of the new science in his previous arguments. 

T3:
	From Dioptrique Section
	Establishing Descartes belief that mediation from sense into knowledge is a physical process through the nerves. 

T4:
	"This is true not only of colors but of vision"
	Descartes makes an analogy to the picture and argues that light forms an image in us which resembles the object, the same way a picture resembles an object. Sustaining the "image" as an ontological object. Burton and Kepler used the image to argue for the former's relation to the object (either through intentional species or any other subtype of authentic transmission), Descartes turns it into light. 

T5:
	The causal effects (light), being regular and orderly, will produce outcomes that "always have some truth to it" 167. Therefore, the representations cannot be any more true or false than others.
