Purpose: To go over the different interpretations of Descartes' hyperbolic doubt. Specifically, how the common interpretations are different to how this doubt served Descartes. 

Body and Soul [Traditional Interpretation]
Following from the alleged, strict, division between body and soul. Soul not being able to capture physical relations -> hence not being able to capture objects

Skepticism [Popkin's Intepretation]
Descartes' hyperbolic doubt as a rediscovery of Phrrhonism. A restating of ancient skeptical arguments 

Arguments are being borrowed, but the conclusions and applications differ from the ancient scenarios. 

Certainty and the Veil of Ideas [Modern Anxiety]
Bernard William' all knowledge must carry mathematical-like certainty. 

Rorty shows how different this worry is from Ancient Skeptics. The "veil of ideas"

The Effective Doubt
Descartes brings forward this hyperbolic doubt only to reject it.
