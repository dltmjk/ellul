T1: 
	Traces Descartes' encounters with Kepler and optics 

T2: 
	'What is clear' 
	Kelperian optics informed Descartes' inquiry into perception as causal. 
T3: 
	'The very first words'
	Comparison with musical perception as a causal process. The causal nature of hearing is different from the subjective cognitive process of meaning attribution. 
T4: 
	'Mersenne concludes that'
	The senses do not deliver 'judgements', such as sweetness or any type of meaning.
	Music is the perspective of hearing. Perspective is the music of the eye. I.e., Sense data is specific to the organ which acts an instrument for it. 
T5: 
	'A decade later'
	Descartes applying these ideas to his work 'Regulae'
T6: 
	'Descartes' introduction of the seal-and-wax'
	Descartes' inversion of the seal and the wax metaphor from Aristotle. Turns into development of representation vs re-presentation. Representation vs signification. 

MP1: If we do not receive any information about the object and only shapes impressed upon our eyes, how do we have knowledge of objects and how do we construct this knowledge. 

$\therefore$ Descartes proposes relations whose relationship with the subject is through the orderliness of causal relations and not of representation of the object. The locus of material order: Shape. Same causal relations means that we can have reliable knowledge without any likeness.
