Plato also speaks of signets being impressed at Theaetetus 191cd (ὥσπερ δακτυλίων σημεῖα ἐνσημαινομένους, d8),
a passage frequently cited as a parallel for de An. 2.12. But Plato is describing memory there and
the impressions are taken from perceptions and thoughts (ταῖς αἰσθήσεσι καὶ ἐννοίαις, Tht.
191d7), not perceptible objects, as in de An. 2.12. It thus has more in common with Aristotle’s use
of the signet ring comparison for memory at Mem. 1, 450a27–32. See the discussion below
