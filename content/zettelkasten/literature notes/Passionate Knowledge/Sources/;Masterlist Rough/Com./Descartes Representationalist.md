On the discussion if Cartesian Sensations are Representationalist

For an overview of the different types of Cartesian Sensations; see Nelson (2013) and Smith (2005). 

Margaret Wilson (1990) argues that Cartesian Representationalism arose out of a need to surpass the a previous resemblance theory.  

See Simmons (1999) for the motivation of a representationalist theory within Descartes. 

For a mechanism of sensations; see Keating (1999). Arbini (1983) argues that Descartes' only coherent formulation of sense-perception is in his scientific, not philosophical works. For a study of a representation in the Dioptrique; see MacKenzie (1990). 

[[Mechanism and the Representational Nature of Sensation in Descartes; Keating 1999]]

For arguments that Descartes does not hold a representationalist theory of perception; See Yolton (1984); Cook (1987); Lennon (1974); Nadler (1989); and Devine (1993)
