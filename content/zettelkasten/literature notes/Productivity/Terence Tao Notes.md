## [Work Hard](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/career-advice/work-hard/)
1. Read all relevant literature, write up a complete detailed treatment of the topic after making a sketch. 
2. Work when all of these categories are fulfilled. 
	1. Motivated
	2. Energetic
	3. Prepared 
	4. Free from distraction

## [Learn and Relearn your Field](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/career-advice/learn-and-relearn-your-field/) 
1. Can you find alternative ways of arguing this point? 
2. Why is each point of the argument necessary? 
3. Which of your points are known? What is new? Focus on these in a separate way 
	1. Are there ways your point can be made in a simpler way? 
	2. When you do make a new point, what needs to be explained and what can be taken for granted? 
4. What are some good examples. Is this analogous to any other areas in different fields? 
5. How does this fit into the wider paradigm 
	1. How would this be used
	2. What are the most important parts 
	3. Is there a simpler formulation? 

## [Time Management](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/2008/08/07/on-time-management/)
1. Do not drop a task when it is only partially finished 
	1. Separate your projects into chunks that are finish-able 
2. Tasks that require little concentration should be done in batches 
3. Spend time learning skills that you will use repeatedly in the future 
4. Make a real effort to stick to your organizational system. 
	1. Let the system evolve over time but start with a basic one and stick to it. 

## [Rapid Prototyping](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/advice-on-writing-papers/write-a-rapid-prototype-first/)=
1. Write the skeleton of the paper
	1. Get the big picture right 
2. Write down the big steps of the argument, what you will be citing, where the argument will come from  
3. Carefully write the key portions of the argument 
4. Fill in the remaining gaps of the paper 

## [On Reading ](https://terrytao.wordpress.com/advice-on-writing-papers/on-compilation-errors-in-mathematical-reading-and-how-to-resolve-them/)
1. Read ahead a few lines to get what the author is talking about 
2. If there is a cryptic comment you can't figure out, ignore it and move on
