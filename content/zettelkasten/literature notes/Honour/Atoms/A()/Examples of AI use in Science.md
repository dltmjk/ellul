[[On the Opacity of Deep Neural Networks]]
	"Deep neural networks (DNNs) are, by now, familiar to most scientists. They are used across all fields of science, for example, to screen for anthrax spores (Jo et al., 2017), to calculate phenotypic distances from butterfly photographs (Cuthill et al., 2019), to improve breast cancer diagnostics (Witowski et al., 2022), to monitor urban earthquakes (Yang et al., 2022), in materials science (Zhong et al., 2022), and so on."
