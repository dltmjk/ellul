\noindent Within Herodotus, Kyros says the following: 

\begin{quote}
I have never yet feared such men, for whom there is a place appointed in the middle of the city for them to come together and deceive each other on oath \footnote{Hdt. 1.153.1}.

\begin{flushright}
τοῖσί ἐστι χώρος ἐν μέσῃ τῇ πόλι ἀποδεδειγμένος ἐς τὸν συλλεγόμενοι ἀλλήλους ὀμνύντες ἐξαπατῶσι
\end{flushright}
    
\end{quote}

\noindent Herodotus Continues: 

\begin{quote}
all the Greeks, because, having set up agoras, they practice buying and selling\footnote{Hdt. 1.153.2}.

\begin{flushright}
ὅτι ἀγορὰς στησάμενοι ὠνῇ τε καὶ πώλη χρῶνται
\end{flushright}
    
\end{quote}


\noindent According to Diogenes Laertius, Anacharsis 

\begin{quote}
    defined the agora as a place for deceiving each other and claiming more than one’s share \footnote{D.L. 1.105}
    \begin{flushright}
τὴν ἀγορὰν ὁρισαμένου ἔφη τόπον εἶς τὸ ἀλλήλους ἀπατᾶν καὶ πλεονεκτεῖν \end{flushright} 
\end{quote}
