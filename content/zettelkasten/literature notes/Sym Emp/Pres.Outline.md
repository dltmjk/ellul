The nature of our relationship with fiction is informed by our childhood construction of mental models through the reading of fiction. Our emotional responses towards fiction arises how we view ourselves in relation to this type of "make-believe". If imaginary friends are the exploration of counterfactuals and behaviour. Then fiction is the exploration of worlds. 

What is our relationship with "make-believe". Is it us pretending that those emotions are ours. How does a child view himself in relation to the examples that build his mental models. What is his connection to his imaginary friends? What is his connection with fiction? 

What is our own relationship with characters in fiction? 

Fiction and imaginary scenarios follow from our ability to create counterfactuals. It seems like the role of emotions is deeply tied to the role of counterfactuals in our reasoning and decision-making. Children are aware of the "imaginary nature" yet allow it to affect them. Likewise adults are aware of the "fictional" nature of novels, yet there is an adverse emotional reaction. 

Our emotions towards fiction are not quasi-emotional. According to Walton, fiction is an imaginative exploration of worlds. This corresponds with the child's imagining.
