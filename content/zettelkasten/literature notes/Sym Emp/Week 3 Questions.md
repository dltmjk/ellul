Dinishak raises the idea that there are some mental features of autists that a non-autist could not re-create or model through simulation. Given that they seem to interpret the world in a qualitatively different way. Given this, how should a non-autist create a framework to conceptualise an autists beliefs and rational opinions. Especially if some of these beliefs are un-graspable for the non-autist? How are these considerations meant to be applied given this schism? Should non-autists use analogous parts of their own mental model in an attempt to create an intermediary for autists or does this also present accompanying danger? 

This can be extended in the Rueda et al's paper where they state that "[the deficit in recognizing positive emotions] relate[s] to the ability of people with AS to share these emotions? If they are given the right information, could they empathise with others’ positive emotions". If autistic and non-autistic models are not always open to simulation, then what form does "the right information" take?
