What molecule do cells use to release and store energy?  
ATP (adenosine triphosphate)

Why are cells considered living?  
Because they reproduce, grow, feed, move, excrete waste, and respond to changes in their environment.

What do all cells have in common?  
Plasma membrane and cytoplasm containing ribosomes and DNA.

What is the function of the plasma membrane?  
Movement of materials into and out of the cell.

What is found inside the plasma membrane?  
Cytoplasm, which contains specialized bodies called organelles.

What is contained within the organelles of a cell?  
DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid), the molecule that stores genetic information.

What are the two main types of cells?  
Prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.

What does “eukaryote” mean?  
From Greek: _eu_ = proper, _karyon_ = nucleus → “true kernel/nucleus”.

What does “prokaryote” mean?  
From Greek: _pro_ = before, _karyon_ = kernel/nucleus → “before nucleus”.

How big is a typical prokaryotic cell?  
1–10 micrometers

How big is a typical eukaryotic cell?  
10–100 micrometers

What is the basic structure of a prokaryote?  
Cell wall, plasma membrane, capsule, flagellum, DNA (loop), and ribosomes.

Is prokaryotic DNA enclosed in a membrane?  
No – prokaryotes have no membrane-bound nucleus.

Describe prokaryotic DNA.  
It’s looped and not associated with histone proteins.

Describe eukaryotic DNA.  
It’s linear, organized into chromosomes, and combined with histones to form chromatin.

Is eukaryotic DNA enclosed?  
Yes, it's inside a membrane-bound nucleus.

How do prokaryotes divide?  
By binary fission, a simple process with no spindle.

How do eukaryotes divide?  
By mitosis, which involves a spindle apparatus made up of microtubules. Sexual reproduction only occurs in Eukaryotes which involves syngamy and meiosis.

How do prokaryotes reproduce genetically?  
Sexual reproduction is rare; they exchange genetic material via horizontal gene transfer.

How do eukaryotes reproduce sexually?  
Through meiosis to form gametes, followed by fertilization.

How large are prokaryotic ribosomes?  
70S (Svedberg units)

How large are eukaryotic ribosomes?  
80S (Svedberg units)

What is a locomotion structure in cells?  
Structures like flagella or cilia that allow movement.

Describe the locomotion structure of prokaryotes.  
A simple flagellum made of flagellin that moves by rotation.

Describe the locomotion structure of eukaryotes.  
Complex cilia and flagella with a 9+2 microtubule arrangement made of tubulin, moving in a whip-like motion.

Do prokaryotes have a cell wall?  
Yes, but it's chemically different from that of eukaryotes (contains peptidoglycan in bacteria).

Do eukaryotes have a cell wall?  
Only in plant cells (cellulose) and fungal cells (chitin).

How do prokaryotic metabolic pathways function?  
They are varied, often using unusual or anaerobic energy sources.

How do eukaryotic metabolic pathways function?  
They mostly use aerobic pathways, including glycolysis and the Krebs cycle.

What is the Krebs cycle?  
A series of reactions in the mitochondria that produce ATP, CO₂, and electron carriers during aerobic respiration.

What is horizontal gene transfer?  
A process where prokaryotes pass genetic material from one cell to another, not through reproduction.

What determines the lower limit of cell size?  
The amount of DNA needed to program metabolism and produce essential enzymes and cellular components.

What determines the upper limit of cell size?  
Metabolic needs and the cell's ability to exchange gases, nutrients, and waste through the plasma membrane.

Why are small cells more efficient at exchanging materials?  
In small cells, random motion of molecules is sufficient to distribute materials quickly.

How do large cells adapt to maintain efficient exchange?  
They become long and thin or develop projections/foldings to increase surface area-to-volume ratio.

Do prokaryotes have a nucleus?  
No, prokaryotes lack a membrane-bound nucleus.

Where is DNA found in prokaryotes?  
In the cytoplasm as a single circular chromosome, not complexed with proteins.

What are plasmids?  
Small, extra-chromosomal DNA molecules in bacteria that carry a few genes, such as those for antibiotic resistance.

What do F plasmids do?  
They help form conjugation tubes for transferring genetic material between bacteria.

What do R plasmids do?  
They carry genes that can provide antibiotic resistance.

Do eukaryotes have a nucleus?  
Yes, eukaryotes have a membrane-bound nucleus that contains chromosomes made of DNA and proteins.

Do prokaryotes have organelles?  
No, prokaryotes lack membrane-bound organelles.

Do eukaryotes have organelles?  
Yes, eukaryotes have internal compartments and organelles.

Where are ribosomes found in prokaryotes?  
Free-floating in the cytoplasm.

Where are enzymes for cellular respiration located in prokaryotes?  
Bound to the cell membrane.

Where are enzymes for cellular respiration located in eukaryotes?  
In the mitochondria.

Describe the difference between Bacterial Flagella and Eukaryotes  
Bacterial Flagella is simple and composed of a single fibre of protein flagellin and has a spin motion (like a propeller). Whereas Eukaryotic flagella is complex and has a 9+2 structure of microtubules and has a whip like motion.

Where are photosynthesis enzymes located in photosynthetic bacteria?  
Bound to the cell membrane.

Where are photosynthesis enzymes found in eukaryotes?  
In specialized organelles called plastids (e.g., chloroplasts).

How many types of photosynthesis do eukaryotes perform?  
One type – oxygen-producing (oxygenic) photosynthesis.

How many types of photosynthesis do prokaryotes perform?  
Multiple – both aerobic and anaerobic photosynthesis.

What are some end products of prokaryotic anaerobic photosynthesis?  
Sulphur, sulphate, and oxygen, depending on the pathway used.
