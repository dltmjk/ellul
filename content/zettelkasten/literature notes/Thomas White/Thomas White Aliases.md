So, like other English Catholics, he was obliged to adopt a number of aliases - a strategy intended to confuse contemporary authorities, but probably more effective in confusing subsequent historians; and White is, then, variously referred to as 'Anglus', 'Candidus', 'Albius', 'Bianchi', and 'Vitius', and in particular as 'Blacklo' (spelt in different ways), the name that was adopted by his followers, the so-called 'Blackloists'.
