Descartes, within His Meditations, establishes a causal theory of sensations. That is, sensations of objects are causally produced by objects in a passive process in the same way that a clock produces the time. 

He writes "a clock made of wheels and counterweights follows all the laws of nature no less closely
when it has been badly constructed and does not tell time accurately than it does when it
completely satisfies the wish of its maker". Descartes, Meditations, Meditation Six, 47.

If sensations are produced in this manner then they "do not belong within these “classes [of thoughts] … in which … truth or falsity properly reside.”274.
