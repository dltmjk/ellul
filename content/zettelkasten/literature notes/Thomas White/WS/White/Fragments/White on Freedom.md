Humans’ freedom, then, their “indeterminacy,” is not “liberty” from
causes, but the ability to act against their natural interests, as defined by the
passions. In fact, this ability – this freedom – to seek pain and flee pleasure is
the prime evidence to the very existence of the human mind:

[...]

Passion rises from goods or ills to Nature, that is, those things which are
conformable or dissonant to nature: Man, if he be … prompt to embrace any
ill of nature, or fly any good; must of necessity have such a faculty in him, [so]
that, in his Brain, ills of nature greater then any such good may be joyn’d to
the good which he is to eschue, and likewise greater goods to the ill he is to
embrace.91
