## Introduction
1. Assuming time travel is possible and objects endure
2. How is it possible for objects to travel back in time and meet its earlier self? 
	1. Four-dimensionalists have and easier answer to this question  
		1. They hold that objects persist by perduring 
			1. An object is a 'space-time' worm that has a temporal part which is present at a certain time. 
			2. Therefore, if objects are 4-dimensional, objects are differentiated on this temporal part. 
		2. However, three-dimensionalists, or endurantists argue that objects have only three-dimensions  
			1. Therefore, they are wholly present whenever they exist. 
				1. For example, if an enduring object $O$ exists through $T$, then for every time $t$ and $\dot{t}$ in $T$, $O$ wholly exists at $t$ and $\dot{t}$ and is also strictly identical to itself at both of these times. 
					1. Therefore, your younger self and you are strictly identical. 
## Endurantists 
1. Three-dimensional objects are fully present but hold different properties at different points in spacetime. 
	1. Therefore, younger Mary (YS) and older Mary (TT) are just the same Mary under different 'descriptions'
	2. However, this contradicts Leibniz' Law since TT and YS have distinct properties despite being the same object. 
		1. The endurantist responds to this by instituting indexicalism. 
			> object $O$ can be wholly red at $t_1$ and wholly blue at $t_2$, and yet be wholly present and strictly identical at $t_1$ and $t_2$.
