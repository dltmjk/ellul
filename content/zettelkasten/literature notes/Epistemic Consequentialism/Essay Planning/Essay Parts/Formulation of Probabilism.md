## Initial Formulation

Probabilism, Initial Formulation
The most popular rational credence norms are that of Kolmogorov's (1933/1950) axoims. Dubbed the *'probability axioms'*, under these, all of your credences should satisfy the axioms of probability. $\mathcal{F}$ is a $\sigma$-algebra over a non-empty set of possible worlds $\mathcal{W}$ which is closed under negation and countable disjunction (Kolmogorov 1933/1950). 

Let $\text{Cr}_i(\cdot) : \text{F}_i \to \mathbb{R}$ be a function mapping members of $\text{F}_i^t$ to any real number. $\text{Cr}_i(\cdot)$ represents an agent $i$'s credence function in which credences are bounded in the following way $0 \le \text{Cr}_i(\mathcal{X}) \le 1$. A credence of 0 means that the agent is certain that the proposition is false, whereas a credence of 1 indicates that the subject believes the proposition is true. Further constraints on the agent's credences are such: 

1. Non-Negativity 
	1. Credences should not be negative 
	2. For every $\mathcal{X} \in \mathcal{F}$, $\text{Cr}_i(\mathcal{X}) \ge 0$
2. Normalisation
	1. Credences in contradictions should be 0, and truisms should be 1
	2. For every $T \in \mathcal{F}, \text{Cr}_i(T) = 1$
3. Finite Additivity 
	1. Credences in the finite union of disjoint sets is equal to the sum of the credences in the individual sets. 
	2. For mutually exclusive members $\mathcal{X}, \mathcal{Z} \in \mathcal{F}, \text{Cr}_i(\mathcal{X} \lor \mathcal{Z}) = \text{Cr}_i(\mathcal{X}) + \text{Cr}_i(\mathcal{Z})$
