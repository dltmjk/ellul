## 1.1 Causal Expectation Values

Let $W$ be the set of epistemically possible worlds, i.e., ways that the world might be that
cannot be ruled out a priori. We say that a set $P \subseteq W$ is a proposition. We say that a set
of propositions $\mathcal{A}$ is a $\sigma$-algebra just in case $\mathcal{A}$ is closed under complementation and finite and countable unions. And we say that a $\sigma$-algebra $\mathcal{A}$ is atomic just in case $\mathcal{A}$ is such that
there exists some $\text{At} \subset \mathcal{A}$ such that:

1. Each $P \in \text{At}$ is non-empty, 
2. $\text{At}$ partitions $W$, and
3. for each $P \in \text{At}$, there is no non-empty set $Q \in \mathcal{A}$ such that $Q \subset P$.

We’ll say that the members of $\text{At}$ are the atoms of $\mathcal{A}$.

Let $\text{At}_i^t$ be an atomic $\sigma$-algebra of propositions and let  $\text{Cr}_i^t(\cdot) : \text{At}_i^t \to \mathbb{R}$ be a function mapping members of $\text{At}_i^t$ to real numbers. We take $\text{Cr}_i^t(\cdot)$ to represent an agent $i$'s credence at some time $t$. The members of $\text{At}_i^t$ are those propositions that $i$ is able to entertain at $t$, and $\text{Cr}_i^t(\cdot)$ represents how likely $i$ at $t$ thinks it is that each such proposition is true. We
let $\text{Att}_i^t \subset \text{At}_i^t$ be the set of atoms of $\text{At}_i^t$.

Let $u_i^t(\cdot) : W \to \mathbb{R}$ be a function mapping members of $W$ to real numbers. We take $u_i^t(\cdot)$
to represent $i$'s values at $t$. We have, then, that $u_i^t(w) \geq u_i^t(w')$ just in case, given $i$'s values at $t$, $w$ is at least as desirable as $w'$.
