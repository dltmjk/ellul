### Abstract and Introduction 

1. Sketch of ACE
	1. Four Main Theses 
		1. Estimation 
			1. Credences are used to make estimates of truth 
		2. Accuracy 
			1. A credal state is accurate to the extent that it sanctions accurate estimates 
		3. Scoring 
			1. Scoring rule I measures b's accuracy/inaccuracy when $w$ is actual. 
			2. Larger numbers represent greater divergences from the truth 
				1. 0 means perfection 
	2. Final Principle of ACE ties accuracy to evidence 
		> An epistemically rational believer will occupy a credal state that minimises estimated inaccuracy in light of her total evidence. 
2. Norms for ACE 
	1. Accuracy non-dominance 
		1. A credal state is defective whenever there is another state that is more accurate under every possible truth-value assignment. 
			1. Does the agent have to be aware of this alternative credal state? is the former credal state permissible if you are ignorant? 
		2. Chance Estimation (CE) 
			1. In light of known chances (probabilities of how the world will be), we should adopt the credal state C that has lower inaccuracy in this new world, instead of b
		3. Value of Learning 
			1. When one learns a new B, her expected inaccuracy should go down
