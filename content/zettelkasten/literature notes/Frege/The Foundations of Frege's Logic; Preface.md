1. Those, recent, ardent defenders of Frege are those who align themselves against Hilbert. 
	1. However, they are against almost all of the main principles that Frege used within his work
		1. His Platonism - the idea that there are functions, concepts, truth-values and thoughts that are above material objects. 
		2. His Realism - the view that thoughts are an independent entity regardless of their expression in any particular language. 
		3. His view that logic deals with a range of these 'extra-linguistic' entities that are established before any sort of maximization. 
			1. The consequence of this is that, between two alternative logics (similar to two alternative geometries), only one can be correct
			> I beg to be excused from joining the stampede called symbolic logic. Turning logic into the study of an artificial language (which nobody speaks) does not strike me as the height of wisdom. A formula of symbolic logic, just like a piece of musical notation, is utterly uninteresting in its own right. Its interest stems exclusively from its ability to represent something other than itself. - The Foundations of Frege's Logic, Pavel Tichý #quote 
2. What Frege are Russell did in their work on symbolic languages was not to establish symbolic languages as a subject, but instead to use it as a way to discuss extra-linguistic entities
	1. While their theories are now known to be flawed, they are still the most advanced theories of objectual logic we possess. 
3. While Frege's theory is seriously flawed, not just through the formal inconsistencies discovered by Russell in *Grundgesteza* but also further ambiguities contained within the ontological foundation of his system. 
	1. Rehabilitation will be sought in the direction of Russells Ramified Theory of Types
		1. However, this also suffers from a similar flaw: 
		> a failure to devise a viable objectual account of the variable. It was this failure which forced both authors to deviate in crucial points from their own objectual approach and to resort to linguistic ascent. - The Foundations of Frege's Logic, Pavel Tichý #quote
