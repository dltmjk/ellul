"History has a direction" Hobsbawm

Thesis: The welfare state seems to provide both a progressive and coercive nature. It provides needed service but does so in a predatory and authoritarian manner. 

Coercive nature - Authoritative control and squashing of revolutionary potential. 

Point 1: Nature of a capitalist welfare state along with historical example
Welfare is defined as the following:
1. Provision of minimum income for all
2. Provision of income for certain social contingencies 
3. Provision of a certain range of social services 

Welfare state could also be defined as the elaborate edifice of social policy, fiscal structure, state intervention and ideology. 

Start examples on the welfare state: Bismarck, 

UK Universal Credit, recipients are subjected to extensive surveillance, digital monitoring, and sanctioning if they fail to meet work-seeking requirements.

Point 2: Coercive nature of the welfare state 
	Talk about how both of the above cases were coercive. 

Otto von Bismarck introduced social insurance programs (sickness, accident, old age) in Imperial Germany as a strategy to neutralize socialist movements and bolster the ruling order.

instead of guaranteeing dignity, the welfare system manages poverty to maintain labor market discipline, punishing non-workers and ensuring a compliant workforce.

Marxist insight: This is a clear case of welfare being used to co-opt working-class sentiment, not out of altruism, but to preserve capitalist stability.

Point 3: Welfare state relationship contradictory nature with capitalism 
The undermined growth of capital in the face of greater public needs 
“While capitalism cannot coexist with the welfare state, neither can it exist without it.”

This is because it grants minimal concessions to the working class while strictly containing capitalist logic within manageable limits. Therefore it actually stabilises the exploitative nature of capitalism. 

Conclusion:

The welfare state as a mechanism of social control over the working class. Despite its prima facie appearance, it still acts against the interests of the working class. 

Welfare provision 

This account may seem vulgar and conspiratorial. The welfare state was not prepared by the ruling class as a cunning master plan to dupe the workers. However, this is evidence that despite the accounts which plea that the welfare state expresses universal interest in favour of the workers. It is possible to sketch an narrative in which it acts as a mechanism of social control. 

Piven and Cloward, in their book "Poor People's Movements" characterise the U.S's attempts at a welfare state as purposed only to 'regulate the poor'.
