Thesis: The growing scarcity of food, interrelated with the growing sentiment of food quality as a luxury. 

How to make this more unique: Transformation of food into a reproducible commodity with the additions of additives and preservatives. Within 19th century literature of the topic, this was known as the "adulteration" of food. This was the interest of capital, to save costs and ensure constantly sell-able produce. As Marx noted: "The bread of the poor was different to the bread of the rich". 

E.P Thompson characterised three different varieties of bread. "the finer white loaf for the wealthy, the intermediate “‘household’ loaf” for the middle class, and the brown loaf, full of waste, for the poor: “Dark bread was suspect as offering easy concealment for noxious additives" 

These issues are still relevant. Food quality, at least within the US, is only maintained by stringent federal regulations. But chemicals added to enhance color, flavor, or storage properties remain ubiquitous, and toxins in food, resulting from the introduction into the environment of some 80,000 new synthetic chemicals, not the product of evolution, are a major concern. Nestle, the worlds largest food company recently admitted that more than 60% of it's products do not meet the 'recognised standards of health'. 

Food production is the ultimate contradiction of capitalism. UK supermarkets throw away 200 million meals of food each year, while the global poor face constant food security issues. This is a necessary consequence of market logic. i.e. the production of profit.
