Thesis: Feminism. What is feminism actually? 

Western feminist progress seems to have halted. Advances in thought, from a backwater 18th century conception of women, has led to genuine material revolutions. Freedom to vote, to a higher education, to enter the majority of professions. A narrowing pay-gap. Yet uncertainty looms over the current strain of western feminism, a sense that there is still unfinished business. 

Female empowerment cunningly turned into a marketing tool. Potentially distracting from real revolutionary change. In their ambiguity, the nature of feminism has muddled itself. Feminism has be co-opted by capitalism. The only way to overcome this is to address the actual material underpinnings on feminism. 

Example, dichotomy of sex work. Feminism in the colonial - or third world. 

Feminism as an advance of real materialist interests. Not simply for the woman to occupy the same place as the man. Colonialist feminism. However, does this entrench further Western ideas? acting as a sort of cultural imperialism.
