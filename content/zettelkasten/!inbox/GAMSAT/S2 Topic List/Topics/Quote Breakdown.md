Retie with narrative: Silence 

Contemporary example: Noise in modern life. 

Do you ever notice how everywhere you go, there's now music. Malls, restaurants, even bookshops? There doesn't seem to be an escape, the sound bleeds into the public. Into the parks. Coming to grips with yourself means silence. You have to lock yourself up - and think. It's not easy and I'm no expert. I know what it takes but I still routinely fail. The toys of modern life are far too attractive, they assail you at every corner, and I am as impressionable as the rest. The rise of noise is inseparable from the rise of globalisation. Subsequently, the rise of the internet, of short form media, of a magic metal box in everybody's pockets. 

Marshall Mcluhan, a Canadian media studies scholar, coined the term "The Medium is the Message". He did this back in the 60s, which makes his work awfully prescient.  

It's hard to analyse yourself because it's so easy to be distracted, because you're viewed from a thousand different places. Satre put it best "Hell is other people", now you're in hell even if you don't know it. Social media makes it so you're routinely fashioning an image of yourself, remaking and retooling as to never appear stale. The focus is now on the presentation of things, so no wonder the interior is left to rot. 

For the first 2 decades of my life, I never really thought about myself. About who I was and my subconscious makeup. Instead, I was absorbed into a world of endless noise. My life became a foray into various modes of distraction. It took me a while to realise that life was more than its external manifestations. A world like ours will always pay homage to the image and it's impossible to separate yourself from its grasp. Yet, it's impossible to ignore exactly how much it comes to define us. It's entirely possible that my words will seem blatantly untrue to someone who able to easily combine their internal and external lives. I am not one of those people and I applaud their ability, yet we both still understand the importance of trying to understand yourself. It's a responsibility we hold ourselves to. One is one where we escape this mindless world of simulation and sound. Into something personal, into something we create.
