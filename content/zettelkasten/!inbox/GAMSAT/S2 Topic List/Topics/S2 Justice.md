Justice

it seems to me that the idea of justice in itself is an idea which in effect has been invented and put to work in different types of societies as an instrument of a certain political and economic power or as a weapon against that power

What is counted as justice depends on those who are the power to convict. Normative justifications for what counts as justice and not is decided by the interests of the state. 

Justice serves the interests of the people with the power to enforce violence. The state, as an apparatus with a monopoly on violence, is the only one capable of doing this. Their interests, that of capital, is served. 

Justice is material. It serves specific material interests.

Cause: A state maintains its power through the maintaining of certain material realities. For example, military or economic might. Therefore, they will use their resources to protect these conditions. 

Therefore, anything that can threaten these interests is reprimanded harshly. 

Consequences: 

Cause: If a country maintains its power through military might, it will expend every in protecting that. Justice is carried out in defence of those things. 


Corruption in democracy: Alan Badiou draws a distinction between two types (or rather levels) of corruption in democracy: the first, empirical corruption, is what we usually understand by the term, but the second pertains to the form of democracy per se, and the way it reduces politics to the negotiation of private interests

e.g. lobbying, interest groups, compromises between competing self-interests

A rare honest democratic politician might fight against empirical corruption (exposing bribery, refusing shady deals) while still fully operating within and sustaining the structural corruption (treating politics as nothing more than bargaining between private interests). 

If democracy is a representation, it is the representation of capitalism 

‘If democracy means representation,’ Badiou writes in _De quoi Sarkozy est-il le nom?_, ‘it is first of all the representation of the general system that bears its forms. In other words: electoral democracy is only representative in so far as it is first of all the consensual representation of capitalism, or of what today has been renamed the “market economy”. This is its underlying corruption.’[*](https://www.lrb.co.uk/the-paper/v31/n14/slavoj-zizek/berlusconi-in-tehran#)
