Arthur Worthington, in attempting to classify the fluid mechanics of a droplet hitting a surface, would rely on images left on his retina of mercury hitting a clean glass plate. In doing so, he produced seemingly symmetrical images meant to mimic the "harmony" of nature. 

![[Pasted image 20250325105522.png]]

It was not until Spring of 1894 where he succeeded in capturing a photograph of the droplets splash, however the image contained none of the symmetry previously assumed. Eventually forcing Worthington to assume that the "reality" of nature was asymmetrical and irregular instead of his previously held "idealizations"
