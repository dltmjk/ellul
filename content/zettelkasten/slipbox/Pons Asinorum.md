From medieval Latin meaning 'Bridge of Asses'. Used as a term to describe the point at which most learners fail. Also a method for finding the middle term of a syllogism in Aristotle. 

Used to denote the fifth proposition in the first book of Euclid. Generally considered a hard midpoint within the work. 

Also used to describe a theorem that the angels opposite the equal sides of an isosceles are also equal.
