Heidegger was a German philosopher most known for his work "Being and Time". He was born 26th September 1889 in Baden. Raised Roman Catholic and within religious schools, he originally intended to become a Jesuit priest. However, he was unable to complete his training due to heart troubles which continued for the rest of his life. 

His main influences were the Southwestern Neo-Kantian school and Husserl. The later of which was his mentor and close friend until his involvement in the Nazi party. His phenomenology was, in a certain way, an improvements on Husserl's system.
