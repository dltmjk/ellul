Niklas Luhmann is a German sociologist and the inventor of systems theory. He argues that social systems are to be understood as communication contexts that have a certain type of autonomy regardless of the agents that inhabit it. 

With this in mind he proposes three types of social systems 
1. Distinguished 
2. Interaction
3. Organization/Society

Modern society is fractured and divided into autonomous *functional areas* (e.g. Law, Politics, Economics) which cannot be coordinated through a central system. 

He also invented the [[Zettelkasten]] system.
